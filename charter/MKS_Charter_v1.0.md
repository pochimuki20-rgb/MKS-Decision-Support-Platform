# MKS Charter v1.0

## MKS Project Charter

**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Approved  
**Scope:** MKS Project / MKS Horse AI / MKS Decision Support Platform

---

## Preamble

MKS is not a system whose final purpose is merely to predict which horse will win.

MKS is designed to support rational decision-making under uncertainty in horse racing.

This Charter defines the highest-level principles for MKS Project, MKS Horse AI, and MKS Decision Support Platform.

---

## Principle 1: Decision Support First

MKS does not treat race prediction as the final objective.

MKS supports decisions such as buying, skipping, selecting bet types, allocating funds, and reviewing results.

---

## Principle 2: Process Over Results

Predictions are evaluated by process, not only by results.

Hit or miss is not the only quality metric. The following must also be evaluated:

- Reasoning quality
- Evaluation process
- Use of information
- Ability to identify improvement points

---

## Principle 3: Continuous Improvement

A wrong prediction is not failure.

Failure is being unable to explain why a prediction was wrong or being unable to use the result for future improvement.

All race results are treated as learning data for process improvement.

---

## Principle 4: Explainability

Every prediction and decision must have reasons.

The system must explain ability, pace, suitability, expected value, and risk where relevant.

---

## Principle 5: Expected Value

MKS prioritizes long-term expected value and return rate over short-term hit rate.

Following popularity rankings is not the goal.

Skipping a race is a valid decision when expected value is low.

---

## Principle 6: Human Centered

The final decision always belongs to the user.

MKS supports judgment; it does not replace human judgment.

---

## Principle 7: Evidence Based Development

New rules and corrections must be adopted based on race review, evidence, and validation.

Do not promote ideas to official specification only because they sound plausible.

---

## Principle 8: Stable Architecture

Separate principles, architecture, specifications, and parameters.

- Charter: stable values
- Architecture: version-controlled structure
- Specification: improvement target
- Parameters: continuously tuned values

---

## Quality Policy

MKS defines quality as:

- Consistent judgment
- Explainable reasoning
- Identifiable improvement points
- Continuous improvement capability
- User confidence in decision-making

---

## Definition of MKS

MKS is not a horse racing prediction system.

MKS is a Horse Racing Decision Support System.
